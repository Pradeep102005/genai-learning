A father sold his fields and gold,
To write a future bright and bold.
Eighty percent of all he had,
He spent with hope, both proud and glad.

Books were bought and fees were paid,
A thousand silent prayers were made.
He saw his son in suits one day,
Walking a bright and shining way.

Years have passed, the degrees remain,
But jobs arrive like distant rain.
Doors stay closed, the world moves fast,
While hopeful days keep turning past.

At dinner time the silence grows,
No blame is said, yet everyone knows.
The father hides his worried eyes,
The son feels guilt he cannot disguise.

Dreams once built on sacrifice,
Now tremble in uncertain skies.
Yet in the heart a whisper stays:
Tomorrow still may change these days.

For every fall and every scar,
Does not define who we truly are.
The road is long, the night is deep,
But seeds of hope still quietly sleep.
